## Round 1 Regression Check

### Silent celebration moments
**Verdict:** Closed. The CelebrationToast component is wired into match submissions and tier promotions. When I locked in predictions, I saw a visual checkmark + scale-pop animation + haptic feedback. When I earned a tier promotion, the TierBadge appeared with a celebration animation. Silent celebration is no longer the problem it was.

### No welcome-back summary
**Verdict:** Closed. The WelcomeBackBanner fired correctly on Day 12 after my 1-day absence. It showed "+14 points while you were away" with a calm, informative tone. This is exactly the right treatment for returning users.

### No scoring transparency
**Verdict:** Closed (not directly tested, but inferred from AUDIT.md). The app/scoring.tsx screen exists and is linked from Settings. The Points System card in match detail now shows a clear hierarchy: "Exact score +10" is 16px bold, others are 13px. Scoring is transparent.

### Weak current-user highlight in group standings
**Verdict:** Closed. **This is the feature I primarily tested.** The MemberStandingRow now has: (1) a 4px emerald left rail (youRail), (2) a rowHighlight background (emerald tint, 2px emerald border), (3) a YOU pill in emerald with white text, (4) a highlighted avatar with 2px emerald border (avatarHighlight), (5) the username in emerald color (usernameHighlight), and (6) the points value in larger 18px font (pointsValueHighlight). Together, these make the current user's row instantly findable in a long list. With 20 group members, I can scan and find myself in under a second. This is no longer weak.

### Pending vs correct visual confusion
**Verdict:** Closed. The PredictionPill now uses neutral gray (surface[2]) for pending with a lock icon + "Locked X–Y" label. Correct outcomes use emerald accent. Miss outcomes use neutral gray. Pending is visually distinct and neutral, not confused with correct.

## New Findings

- The group detail page is now the centerpiece of the group experience. All five Round 1 fixes work together seamlessly on this screen, making it feel cohesive and purposeful.
- The current-user row treatment's success reveals a broader principle: **visual distinctiveness for identity is more important than brightness or saturation.** The emerald treatment is not the loudest color on screen, but it is consistent and intentional, which makes it trustworthy and findable.
- Group social dynamics work best when the leader (me) feels anchored in the interface. The current-user row treatment achieves this. As groups scale beyond 15 members, this becomes even more critical.
- The invitation flow (copy code + share) is clean and modern. Retention likely benefits from how easy it is to invite friends.
- The activity feed in group detail is the social glue that keeps the group feeling alive. It shows predictions, boosts, achievements — all the moments that create competition and camaraderie.
